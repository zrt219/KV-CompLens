UMATTR Career Circle Resource Guide

Support document for athletes after the workshop

Purpose

This guide supports student-athletes after the workshop with practical next steps for AI use, career readiness, personal brand, networking, and life after sport.

Recommended AI Uses

  • Brainstorming career options
  • Drafting and improving resumes
  • Practicing interview answers
  • Planning weekly schedules
  • Writing professional emails
  • Reflecting after setbacks or transitions

Career Readiness Checklist

  • [ ] I know at least three careers I want to explore.
  • [ ] I have a resume draft.
  • [ ] I have a LinkedIn profile or professional bio.
  • [ ] I can explain how my athletic skills transfer to work.
  • [ ] I have sent at least one networking message.
  • [ ] I have spoken with a career center, mentor, coach, or advisor.

Resume Basics

  • Use action words: led, organized, trained, collaborated, managed, competed, adapted.
  • Use numbers where possible: hours trained, years competed, team size, leadership roles, events supported.
  • Connect sport skills to workplace skills.
  • Keep it honest, clear, and easy to read.

Interview Basics

Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

  • Prepare a story about leadership.
  • Prepare a story about failure or adversity.
  • Prepare a story about teamwork.
  • Prepare a story about time management.

Mental Wellness Reminder

This program is educational and not clinical therapy. Athletes should always use campus mental-health, medical, academic, compliance, or counseling supports when needed. UMATTR supports learning, reflection, habits, and personal growth.

UMATTR Career Circle Next Steps

  • Attend the live 50-minute workshop.
  • Complete your workbook and worksheets.
  • Join the online community discussions.
  • Book follow-up coaching, consulting, or team sessions where available.
