# Product

## Register

product

## Users

KV Capital reviewers, hackathon judges, and AI engineering hiring reviewers evaluating a focused underwriting-support workflow. The primary user is an analyst reviewing a subject Alberta residential property and deciding whether the comparable-sales support is credible enough to inform a value range.

## Product Purpose

KV CompLens retrieves, ranks, adjusts, and explains synthetic Alberta comparable residential sales for a subject property. Success means the user can see why each comp was selected, how scores and adjustments affect valuation, what risks remain, and what facts support the underwriting memo.

## Brand Personality

Precise, disciplined, credible. The product should feel like professional underwriting software: clear, inspectable, calm under scrutiny, and focused on decision support rather than chatbot novelty.

## Anti-references

Do not look like a generic real estate chatbot, marketing landing page, fake startup dashboard, low-contrast gray SaaS template, or decorative map demo. Avoid unsupported MLS claims, invented production usage, gamified HUD noise, and vague AI copy.

## Design Principles

- Explain the valuation path: every estimate should be traceable to comps, scores, adjustments, and risk flags.
- Keep the analyst in the workflow: primary actions should support comp review, adjustment inspection, and memo generation.
- Use data density with restraint: show enough detail for underwriting review without burying the signal.
- Treat demo boundaries honestly: local synthetic data and deterministic logic must be labeled clearly.
- Prioritize accessibility: status meaning must use text, icons, shape, and contrast, not color alone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast. The user has severe colorblindness needs, so no status or quality signal may rely on color alone. Use explicit labels, icons, border treatments, and readable contrast. Respect reduced-motion preferences and keep product motion short and state-driven.
